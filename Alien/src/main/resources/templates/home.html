üéì F-1 Student Visa

The F-1 visa is a non-immigrant visa that allows international students to study in the United States at accredited colleges, universities, or other academic institutions. To apply for an F-1 visa, a student must first get admission from a U.S. school and receive an I-20 form, which is required to submit the visa application.

What You Need to Know

Applicants must demonstrate that they have sufficient funds to cover tuition and living expenses, maintain a minimum GPA (usually 2.0 for undergraduates and 3.0 for graduates), and intend to return to their home country after completing their studies.

Students may also participate in extracurricular activities, internships, or research projects, which can enhance their profile. After completing their studies, F-1 visa holders may be eligible for Optional Practical Training (OPT), which allows them to work in their field of study in the U.S. for a limited period.

How to Apply

1
Receive admission from a U.S. school and get the I-20 form.
2
Pay the SEVIS fee and complete the DS-160 visa application.
3
Schedule and attend the U.S. embassy or consulate interview, providing financial documents and academic records.
4
Wait for visa approval and travel to the U.S. to begin studies.

üåü O-1 Extraordinary Ability Visa

The O-1 visa is a non-immigrant visa for individuals who have demonstrated extraordinary ability in fields such as science, arts, education, business, or athletics. Applicants must provide evidence of their achievements, which can include national or international awards, published research, patents, media coverage, high-level employment, or leadership roles.

What You Need to Know

There is no GPA requirement for O-1 visas, as eligibility is based on accomplishments and recognition in the applicant's field.

O-1 visa holders may also submit recommendation letters and proof of their role in judging competitions or contributing to important projects. The visa is usually sponsored by a U.S. employer or agent, who files a petition on behalf of the applicant.

How to Apply

1
Gather evidence of extraordinary ability in your field.
2
Have a U.S. employer or agent file the Form I-129 petition with USCIS.
3
Attend a U.S. embassy or consulate interview, if required.
4
Upon approval, travel to the U.S. to work in your specialized field.
